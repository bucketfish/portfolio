Tambourine
Story

Platform: iPadOS

Tools: Swift, SwiftUI

Team size: Solo

Duration: 2-3 months

Source code: Github


Tambourine Story was created for Swift Student Challenge 2024. It's a rhythm game — but instead of tapping the screen like most rhythm games, it asks the user to shake the iPad like a real-life tambourine.

This game is my tribute to music and collaboration — how each and every one of us are able to be a part of something greater than ourselves, through collaborative art, through playing together.

The project won Distinguished Winner in Swift Student Challenge and was featured on various platforms, such as ChannelNewsAsia, ELLE Singapore, and the App Store.

The project was also presented to Tim Cook when he visited Singapore in April 2024, as well as at Apple Developer Center Singapore.

The story follows a little girl's journey of trying to join her brother's band, and the player shares her experience of being included in the practice session for the first time.

I was inspired to create this game by a band that I started with my friends a few years ago and the wondrous feeling I experienced when we had our rehearsal for the first time, hearing the song come together. It felt like we were a part of something special. I created Tambourine Story to capture this feeling I had, of the human nature of yearning for arts and harmony.

The physical motion of the device was inspired by Phyphox, an app used for Physics Practical lessons in school to record device motion for data analysis purposes.

Technical details

Created entirely in Swift and SwiftUI, Tambourine Story draws on the potential of CoreMotion and SceneKit to make the game more immersive and interactive.

The tambourine was modeled using Blender and imported using SceneKit to be rendered into the game in its 3D form.

CoreMotion uses the device's accelerometer to detect shakes of the tambourine, as well as tilting of the camera to rotate the scene.

Art details

Drawn with Clip Studio Paint, the characters in the game have many emotions to represent them during dialogue. The base — default smile — was drawn first, then the other emotions iterated upon.

The main character was then animated for the jumping animation when the tambourine is played. The inspiration from this animation comes from Thomas the Tank Engine lyric video, with a ball jumping across the lyrics.

The background also took a significant amount of time as I was unhappy with the first version of it. It took some re-painting to get the correct feel of the game down, but it was definitely worth it in the end.

Final version

The music was composed in Garageband. The first piece is simple and short — it loops to ensure that the player knows how to play and can hit a minimum accuracy before continuing. The second begins build up in intensity as the game progresses.

The finale challenge is longer, with all instruments coming in to simulate the feeling of being in a band with many others.
